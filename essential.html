Traditional cuisine and famous historical monuments

Featuring a dash of Mediterranean influence, as well as a significant contribution from the Ottoman cuisine, the Bulgarian dishes are known to be very delicious and at the same time affortable. These are some of the traditional dishes that could be found in almost every restaurant:

  • Banitsa - layered filo pastry with cheese

  • Tarator - creamy cold soup made from yogurt and cucumbers

  • Kiselo Mlyako - meant to be one of the best types of yogurt due to the 2 probiotic types of bacteria in it

  • Moussaka - similar to Greek moussaka with the difference that instead of using egg plant it's potatoes.

  • Shopska salad - salad made with cucumbers,tomatoes,onions,peppers and grated white cheese

Saint Alexander Nevsky Cathedral is the most important Orthodox church in Bulgaria and among one of the 10 largest Eastern Orthodox churches. The cathedral embodies the memory of the sacrifices given for Bulgaria's liberation from Ottoman rule in 1879.

National Theatre Ivan Vazov is the oldest and most authoritative theatre in Bulgaria and one of the capital's most important and fascinating landmarks. It's named after a famous bulgarian writer. It has been a host to multiple productions from notable theatre directors.

National Palace of Culture is the largest multifunctional complex in south-eastern Europe. It was officially opened in honour of 1300-year anniversary of the establishment of the Bulgarian state . The palace hosts different types of events throughout the year.

Vitosha Boulevard is the most popular pedestrian street in Bulgaria. It's filled with many international shops, restaurants, bars and cafes. It's named after one of the mountain surrounding the capital - Vitosha.
